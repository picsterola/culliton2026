The 2026 Washington Supreme Court races, viewed through one question.

Five seats on the Washington Supreme Court are on the 2026 ballot. Most voter guides cover endorsements and biographies. This one isolates a single constitutional question that quietly shapes nearly every major tax-policy debate in the state, and asks what the public record tells us about how each candidate is likely to approach it.

The Underlying Question

Should Culliton v. Chase — the 1933 decision classifying income as "property" under Article VII §1 of the Washington Constitution — be overruled?

Culliton is the doctrinal reason Washington cannot enact a graduated income tax without a constitutional amendment. The 2023 decision in Quinn v. State upheld the capital gains tax by characterizing it as an excise — and expressly declined to revisit Culliton. Whoever sits on this court for the next eight years will decide whether that workaround holds, expands, or breaks.
